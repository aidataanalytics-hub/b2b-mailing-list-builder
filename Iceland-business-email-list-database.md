## **Legal and compliance**

Email marketing is heavily regulated. Even when targeting only businesses, you must follow anti-spam laws. For example, the **U.S. CAN-SPAM Act** applies to all commercial emails – including B2B. The FTC emphasizes that “The law makes no exception for business-to-business email”, so any marketing email you send to Icelandic businesses from or through U.S. servers must comply (clear subject lines, valid headers, opt-out link, etc.).

As members of the EEA, Iceland companies are covered by the **EU GDPR**. Under GDPR, you must have a lawful basis to email contacts – typically explicit opt-in consent or a documented “legitimate interest”. Importantly, recipients always have the right to opt out: the UK’s Information Commissioner states that *“an individual always has the right to object to processing for the purposes of direct marketing, whatever lawful basis applies.”*. In practice, even if your list is “permitted” by legitimate interest, you must clearly honor unsubscribe requests.

If you email any contacts in Canada or citizens abroad, **Canada’s CASL** also comes into play. CASL requires consent for “Commercial Electronic Messages” (broadly any promo email). Mailchimp’s guide warns that if you “send a CEM to Canadian residents, you need to comply with CASL”. Violations carry heavy penalties: fines up to $1,000,000 per infraction for individuals and $10,000,000 for companies.

In summary, even with an Icelandic B2B email list, you must comply with all relevant laws (GDPR in Europe, CAN-SPAM in the US, CASL in Canada, etc.). Work with vendors who document consent/legal collection of their data, and always include a clear unsubscribe in your emails.
