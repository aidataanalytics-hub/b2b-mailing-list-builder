## **Legal and Compliance Considerations**

Using any email list requires following legal rules:

* **GDPR (EU):** For any contacts in Cyprus (an EU member), GDPR applies. You must ensure each email was collected with consent or legitimate interest and that you only send messages they expect. In fact, the EU Commission advises that before using a third-party list, a business must “demonstrate that the data was obtained in compliance with the GDPR” and only email those who have not opted out. Always include a clear privacy notice and unsubscribe option.

* **CAN-SPAM (US):** If you email U.S. leads or use U.S.-based servers, the CAN-SPAM Act requires truth in headers, a real physical address, and an easy opt-out link. All commercial emails must comply, even B2B messages. Violations carry hefty fines (up to \~$53,000 per email).

* **CASL (Canada):** Canada’s law goes further: CASL requires *express or implied consent* for Commercial Electronic Messages (emails, SMS). Unsubscribe links and sender identification are mandatory. Fines can reach $10M per violation.

In practice, a reputable list provider like LeadsBlue builds compliance in: their Cyprus email contacts are sourced from public or consented information, are regularly refreshed, and come with guidance on usage. (For example, Bancomail notes its Cyprus databases are managed *“in compliance with GDPR guidelines”*.) **Always follow each country’s email rules**. Laws evolve, so consult legal counsel if in doubt. But in general, using a quality list and respecting opt-outs keeps your campaigns safe and effective.
