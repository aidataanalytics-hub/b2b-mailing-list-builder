### **Legal Compliance (GDPR, CASL, CAN-SPAM)**

Email marketing must follow laws in each jurisdiction. In Malta (an EU country), the EU’s **GDPR** applies: you generally need explicit opt-in consent to send marketing emails, and recipients must be able to withdraw consent at any time. This is reinforced by the ePrivacy Directive’s rules on newsletters (requiring opt-in for cold emailing). In practice, this means you should only use email lists where contacts have consented or where a legitimate business interest applies with an easy opt-out.

Beyond EU law, consider international rules if your list crosses borders. **Canada’s CASL** is stricter: it prohibits sending any commercial email without prior express or implied consent. Recipients must actively “opt in” before you email them. **USA’s CAN-SPAM Act** sets basic requirements: every marketing email must include accurate header info, your valid physical address, and a clear unsubscribe option. Violating these (for example, emailing private individuals without consent or hiding unsubscribe links) can lead to heavy fines.

*Always use lists from reputable providers who follow these rules.* For instance, providers like LeadsBlue and Bancomail explicitly state their data is gathered in compliance with GDPR. You should still double-check: ensure every email you send includes an opt-out link and the sender’s address, per CAN-SPAM, and honor all unsubscribe requests. In short, targeted Malta lists are powerful – just make sure to stay on the right side of privacy laws (GDPR for EU, plus CASL/CAN-SPAM if emailing Canadians or Americans).
