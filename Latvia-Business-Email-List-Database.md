### **Legal & Compliance Considerations**

Using a purchased email list also means you must follow email laws and privacy regulations. Major rules include:

* **GDPR (EU):** Since Latvia is in the EU, GDPR applies. This means you need a lawful basis (often **explicit opt-in consent**) to email EU contacts. Simply put, recipients should have agreed to be contacted (or you must show a legitimate interest). GDPR also requires offering an easy way to unsubscribe and respecting data subjects’ rights.

* **CAN-SPAM Act (US):** If you email U.S. recipients or even apply good practices globally, remember CAN-SPAM: every commercial email must include the sender’s postal address and a clear unsubscribe link. Deceptive subject lines or headers are prohibite.

* **CASL (Canada):** Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation is stringent: you need either prior consent or an existing business relationship to send email. If you do email Canadians, you must provide identification information and an unsubscribe mechanism.

In practice, the safest approach is to **follow GDPR rules everywhere**. If your list provider claims GDPR compliance (meaning contacts are collected lawfully), and you include opt-outs and accurate headers, you’ll generally meet CAN-SPAM and CASL requirements. Non-compliance can lead to heavy fines (up to €20M under GDPR, or $51,000 per violation under CAN-SPAM). Therefore, always choose a supplier that emphasizes privacy. For example, LeadsBlue highlights that its lists are compiled “in accordance with international data privacy regulations,” and BoldData notes GDPR/CCPA compliance audited by a data authority.
