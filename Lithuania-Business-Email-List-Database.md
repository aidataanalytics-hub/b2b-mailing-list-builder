## **Legal & Compliance Considerations**

When emailing contacts on these lists, it’s crucial to follow anti-spam and privacy laws. In the EU (including Lithuania), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies. Under GDPR, you need **prior consent** to send marketing emails to individuals, except for existing customers receiving similar-product info. All emails must include an easy opt-out. For example, the EU states: “For direct marketing emails that promote particular brands or products, your prior consent is required… You have the right to object at any time to receiving such direct marketing”. In the US, the CAN-SPAM Act sets rules for commercial email: no misleading headers, clear subject lines, a physical address, and a functioning unsubscribe link. Each violation can incur hefty fines. Canada’s CASL similarly requires express or implied consent to send commercial emails, with penalties for infractions. In practice, this means businesses should use opt-in or legitimate-interest leads and always honor unsubscribe requests. Credible list providers claim GDPR/Spam-law compliance, but buyers must still email responsibly.

**Key references:** The FTC explains CAN-SPAM’s core rules for commercial email (mandatory identification, address, and opt-out options, while EU guidance reiterates that email marketing to EU users generally requires opt-in consent. Always check local regulations (GDPR in EU, CASL in Canada, etc.) when sending any B2B campaign.
